# 1. BRAND STRATEGY & POSITIONING

## 1.1 Positioning Frameworks

### Al Ries & Jack Trout — Positioning Theory
- Positioning = what you do to the MIND of the prospect, not the product
- The 7 Laws: Leadership, Category, Mind, Perception, Focus, Exclusivity, Ladder
- Own ONE word in the prospect's mind (Volvo = safety, FedEx = overnight)
- Strategy by position: Leader reinforces "the original"; #2 positions against leader; Follower finds unclaimed attribute; New entrant creates new category

### Category Design (Play Bigger)
- The majority of market value goes to the category king/queen
- 7 steps: Identify problem → Name category → Blueprint ecosystem → Develop POV → Mobilize org → Lightning strikes → Strike ops
- Category marketing says "the old way is broken" vs product marketing says "here's what we do"
- Examples: Salesforce (CRM in the cloud), HubSpot (Inbound Marketing), Uber (ride-sharing)

### Blue Ocean Strategy
- Stop competing in red oceans; create uncontested market spaces
- ERRC Grid: Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create
- Value Innovation = simultaneous differentiation AND low cost
- Six Paths Framework for reconstructing market boundaries
- Examples: Cirque du Soleil, Yellow Tail Wine, Nintendo Wii

### Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD)
- Customers "hire" products to get a job done — functional, emotional, social dimensions
- JTBD statement: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so that [expected outcome]"
- Four Forces: Push of situation, Pull of new, Anxiety of new, Habit/allegiance
- Famous example: McDonald's milkshake — two different jobs for two different customer segments

### Perceptual Mapping
- 2-axis scatter chart showing how consumers perceive brands on key attributes
- Clusters = overcrowded (red ocean), Gaps = opportunity (blue ocean)
- Build with surveys; update quarterly; use for repositioning decisions

## 1.2 Messaging Architecture

### StoryBrand (Donald Miller) — 7-Part Framework
1. A CHARACTER (customer) who wants something
2. HAS A PROBLEM (villain + external + internal + philosophical)
3. MEETS A GUIDE (your brand — empathy + authority)
4. WHO GIVES THEM A PLAN (3-4 simple steps)
5. CALLS THEM TO ACTION (direct CTA + transitional CTA)
6. HELPS THEM AVOID FAILURE (stakes — loss aversion)
7. AND ENDS IN SUCCESS (transformation — before/after)

**Critical rule:** Customer = hero. Brand = guide (Yoda, not Luke).

### Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder)
- Customer Profile: Jobs + Pains + Gains
- Value Map: Products/Services + Pain Relievers + Gain Creators
- Fit = when pain relievers address biggest pains, gain creators address biggest gains

### Messaging Hierarchy (Pyramid)
- Layer 1: Core Brand Promise (single sentence)
- Layer 2: 3-4 Strategic Pillars (supporting themes)
- Layer 3: Proof Points (stats, testimonials, case studies)

### Tone of Voice Development
- 4 Dimensions: Funny↔Serious, Formal↔Casual, Respectful↔Irreverent, Enthusiastic↔Matter-of-fact
- Define "We are X, not Y" pairs
- Voice is constant; tone adapts by channel and context

### Sonic Branding
- Audio logos achieve significantly greater brand recall than visual alone
- Hall of Fame: Intel (5-note bong), Netflix (ta-dum), McDonald's (ba-da-ba-ba-baa)

## 1.4 Competitive Differentiation

### USP Development
- Must be: True, Unique, Important, Durable
- Hall of Fame: Domino's ("30 min or free"), FedEx ("absolutely, positively overnight"), GEICO ("15 minutes could save 15%")

### Points of Parity vs Points of Difference (Keller)
- POPs = table stakes to compete in category
- PODs = reasons to choose YOU
- Establish POPs first (earn right to compete), then assert PODs

### Competitive Moats
1. Brand Moat (trust, recognition)
2. Network Effect Moat (value grows with users)
3. Switching Cost Moat (expensive to leave)
4. Cost Advantage Moat (structural cost advantage)
5. Niche Moat (market too small for large competitors)

## 1.5 Brand Measurement

### Keller's CBBE Pyramid (bottom to top)
1. Salience (Who are you?)
2. Meaning — Performance + Imagery (What are you?)
3. Response — Judgments + Feelings (What about you?)
4. Resonance (How much connection?)

### Aaker's Brand Equity Model — 5 Components
1. Brand Loyalty
2. Brand Awareness
3. Perceived Quality
4. Brand Associations
5. Proprietary Assets

### Key Brand Health Metrics (categories)
- Loyalty metrics: NPS, retention, repeat purchase
- Awareness metrics: TOMA, spontaneous awareness, prompted awareness, share of voice, share of search
- Financial metrics: Price premium, brand revenue contribution, brand valuation

# 2. GROWTH & PERFORMANCE

## 2.1 Growth Frameworks

### AARRR Pirate Metrics
| Stage | Question | Key Metrics |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Acquisition | How do people find us? | CAC, traffic by channel |
| Activation | Do they get value fast? | Time-to-first-value, activation rate |
| Retention | Do they come back? | DAU/MAU, churn rate |
| Referral | Do they tell others? | K-factor (K>1 = viral) |
| Revenue | Do they pay? | MRR, ARPU, LTV, NRR |

### Sean Ellis 40% Test (Product-Market Fit)
- Survey: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?"
- If 40%+ say "Very disappointed" → PMF exists → scale aggressively
- Below 40% → fix product before spending on growth

### Growth Loops vs Funnels
- Funnels are linear and additive; loops are cyclical and multiplicative
- Types: Viral loop, Content loop, Paid loop, Product loop, Community loop

### Brian Balfour's Four Fits
1. Market-Product Fit (burning problem + better solution)
2. Product-Channel Fit (product built FOR its distribution channel)
3. Channel-Model Fit (unit economics work with chosen channel)
4. Model-Market Fit (how you charge matches how market buys)

### ICE/RICE Scoring
- ICE = Impact x Confidence x Ease (each 1-10)
- RICE = (Reach x Impact x Confidence) / Effort — more rigorous

### North Star Metric
- Single metric capturing core customer value AND business health
- Examples: Airbnb (nights booked), Slack (messages sent), Spotify (time listening)
- Revenue is almost always a poor NSM — it's lagging, not driving

## 2.2 Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

### Hero Section Anatomy (priority order)
1. Headline (6-9 words, value prop, answers "what do I get?")
2. Sub-headline (1-2 sentences expanding)
3. Primary CTA (action verb + benefit, single CTA only)
4. Social proof (logos, user count, rating near CTA)
5. Hero visual (product screenshot, not stock photos)

### Form Optimization
- Fewer fields = higher completion; 1-3 fields optimal
- Progressive profiling: gather data across multiple interactions
- Multi-step forms + progress bars reduce perceived friction
- Social login reduces friction significantly

### Pricing Page Optimization
- 3-tier pricing (Goldilocks Effect — users anchor to middle)
- "Most Popular" badge on target plan
- Annual billing toggle showing savings
- FAQ addressing top 5 objections
- Money-back guarantee displayed prominently

## 2.3 A/B Testing

### Statistical Foundations
- Statistical significance: 95% (p < 0.05)
- Statistical power: 80% (β = 0.20)
- Minimum test duration: 2 weeks (captures weekly seasonality)
- Practical minimum: 100 conversions per variation

### Common Pitfalls
1. Peeking problem (checking results early inflates false positives)
2. Simpson's Paradox (overall trend reverses when segmented)
3. Multiple testing problem
4. Underpowered tests
5. Testing cosmetics instead of mechanisms

## 2.4 Analytics & Attribution

### Multi-Touch Attribution Models
| Model | Credit | Best For |
|-------|--------|----------|
| First Touch | 100% to first | Brand awareness |
| Last Touch | 100% to last | Direct response |
| Linear | Equal across all | Long consideration cycles |
| Time Decay | More to recent | Short sales cycles |
| U-Shaped | 40/40/20 | When first + last both matter |
| Data-Driven | ML-assigned | High-volume conversions |

### UTM Best Practices
- Always lowercase; use underscores; never on internal links
- 5 parameters: source, medium, campaign, content, term
- Enforce naming convention across team

## 2.5 Viral & Referral Mechanics

### Viral Coefficient
- K = invites_per_user x conversion_rate
- K > 1 = true viral growth (exponential)
- Shortening cycle time compounds faster even with same K

### Referral Program Design
- Bilateral rewards (both parties) outperform unilateral
- Instant rewards convert better than delayed

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)
- Mechanics: Freemium, Free trial, Inherent virality, Data network effects, Embedded CTAs
- PQL (Product Qualified Lead) converts at dramatically higher rates than MQL

## 2.6 Retention & Engagement

### Onboarding Optimization
- TTFV (Time to First Value): target under 5 minutes
- In-app checklists boost activation significantly

### Hook Model (Nir Eyal)
- Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment → (loop)
- Internal triggers (emotions) are the goal

### Churn Prevention
- Small churn reductions produce outsized profit gains (Bain)
- Retention is far cheaper than acquisition
- Involuntary churn (payment failures) = significant portion of all churn — fix with dunning

## 2.7 Key Formulas
```
K-factor:           K = invites_per_user x conversion_rate
LTV (simple):       LTV = ARPU / Churn_Rate
LTV:CAC target:     >= 3:1
CAC:                Total S&M Spend / New Customers
Payback target:     < 12 months (SaaS)
NRR target:         110%+ (top quartile)
PMF threshold:      >= 40% "Very Disappointed"
```

# 3. DIGITAL CHANNELS & PAID MEDIA

## 3.1 SEO

### On-Page SEO
- Title tags: 50-60 chars, primary keyword in first 30
- Meta descriptions: 155-160 chars; Problem + Solution + CTA
- One H1 per page with primary keyword
- 3-5 internal links per 1,000 words
- E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness

### Technical SEO
- Core Web Vitals: optimize LCP, INP, CLS (see benchmarks for thresholds)
- Schema markup (JSON-LD): Article, FAQ, HowTo, Product, LocalBusiness
- Mobile-first indexing; HTTPS everywhere
- XML sitemaps auto-generated; robots.txt blocking admin/staging

### Off-Page SEO
- Entity endorsements > link juice counting
- Top strategies: Digital PR, Broken link building, Selective guest posting, HARO
- Relevance > Raw DA; anchor text diversity essential

### Keyword Research
- 5-Workflow: Seed expansion → Difficulty assessment → Competitor gap → Clustering → Intent mapping
- Content clusters: Pillar page (broad) + Cluster pages (subtopics) all interlinked
- Sites with mature clusters rank significantly more keywords

### Local SEO
- 3 pillars: Relevance, Proximity, Prominence
- GBP: Complete every field, photos weekly, posts weekly, respond to all reviews
- NAP consistency across all platforms

## 3.2 Paid Advertising

### Google Ads — Principles
- Performance Max: runs across all Google surfaces via AI
- Asset quality: provide maximum images, videos, headlines, descriptions
- Bidding ladder: Start Maximize Conversions → Target CPA (after sufficient conversions) → Target ROAS
- Budget rule: Daily = 10-15x target CPA

### Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — Principles
- Creative IS the targeting — Meta's AI reads creative visually and semantically
- Advantage+ Shopping: broad audiences, AI budget allocation
- UGC dominates: significantly better CPA than polished studio ads
- Maintain 15-20 active creatives; retire underperformers after reasonable spend
- Conversions API (CAPI) + Pixel required for full attribution

### TikTok Ads — Principles
- Creative-first: algorithm cares about engagement, not targeting precision
- Spark Ads: boost organic posts as paid ads, social proof compounds permanently
- Hook in 0-3 seconds mandatory; majority of top-CTR videos hook in first 3 seconds
- Brands testing high volumes of creative variations/month outperform all others

### LinkedIn Ads — Principles
- Only platform targeting by job title, company name, seniority, skills
- Dominant for B2B social leads
- InMail: send from person not brand page
- Lead Gen Forms convert significantly higher than landing pages

### Programmatic — Principles
- Key DSPs: Google DV360, The Trade Desk, Amazon DSP
- Post-cookie: Contextual targeting renaissance; performing on par with behavioral
- Retargeting tiers: TOFU (30-day all visitors) → MOFU (14-day product viewers) → BOFU (7-day cart abandoners)
- Frequency caps: 5-7/week per user

## 3.3 Social Media Marketing

### Platform Algorithm Mechanics (general principles)
- **TikTok**: Watch time + completion rate most important; every video tested on small audience first
- **Instagram**: Sends/shares to DMs = most weighted signal; Reels best organic reach
- **YouTube**: CTR from impressions + average view duration
- **X/Twitter**: Replies weighted heaviest; link posts demoted; threads dominate
- **LinkedIn**: Native video higher reach vs links; documents/PDFs high engagement

### Influencer Marketing
- Micro-influencers (10K-100K): significantly more engagement per dollar than macro
- Best model: Performance-based (affiliate codes) or Hybrid (flat fee + commission)

## 3.4 Email Marketing

### Core 5 Automated Flows
1. Welcome Series (3-7 emails, 5-14 days)
2. Cart Abandonment (3 emails: 2-4hrs, 24hrs, 72hrs)
3. Post-Purchase (order confirm → shipping → onboarding → review → upsell)
4. Re-Engagement (90+ days inactive: "we miss you" → "last chance" → unsubscribe)
5. Lead Nurture/Drip (B2B: 7-14 emails over 30-60 days)

### Deliverability (Non-Negotiable)
- SPF + DKIM + DMARC all required by major email providers
- Keep spam complaint rate minimal
- Sunset policy: auto-suppress no engagement in 180 days

### Core Principles
- Segmented campaigns massively outperform non-segmented
- Automated sequences outperform one-off blasts

## 3.5 SMS & Messaging
- SMS: extremely high open rates, most read within minutes
- Frequency: 4-8 messages/month max
- WhatsApp Business API: pre-approved Meta templates required
- Push notifications: behavior-triggered dramatically outperform scheduled
- Multi-channel sequence: Push (1hr) → SMS (3hrs) → Email (24hrs) → WhatsApp (3 days)

# 4. CONTENT STRATEGY & COPYWRITING

## 4.1 Copywriting Frameworks

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Best for: Cold audiences, awareness ads, email campaigns

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution
"The most reliable sales formula ever invented" (Dan Kennedy)
Best for: Service businesses, SaaS, high-consideration purchases

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge
Transformation framework; sells the outcome, not the product
Best for: Testimonials, VSLs, landing pages, YouTube ads

### 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push
Appeals to both emotional and logical brain
Best for: Sales pages, cold email, product launches

### QUEST — Qualify, Understand, Educate, Stimulate, Transition
Consultative framework; deepest relationship before the sale
Best for: High-ticket funnels, consulting, webinar closes

## 4.2 Master Copywriters' Principles

### Gary Halbert
- Find the starving crowd first — market selection beats copy
- Curiosity is the #1 buying trigger
- Write to ONE person, not a crowd
- Read copy aloud; handwrite great copy to internalize patterns

### David Ogilvy — 11 Principles
- Headlines are 80% of your budget
- Research is everything
- The consumer is not a moron
- Promise a specific benefit
- Informative copy sells
- The Big Idea sustains campaigns for decades

### Eugene Schwartz — 5 Levels of Awareness
| Level | State | Copy Entry Point |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| 1. Unaware | Don't know they have a problem | Story, curiosity, bold claim |
| 2. Problem Aware | Know the pain, not solution | Lead with problem, empathy |
| 3. Solution Aware | Know solutions, not your product | Benefits, differentiation |
| 4. Product Aware | Know you, haven't committed | USP, overcome objections |
| 5. Most Aware | Know and want your product | Offer, price, CTA directly |

**Rule:** Less aware = more work before making an offer.

### Joseph Sugarman — 24 Psychological Triggers
Key triggers: Involvement/Ownership, Honesty, Credibility, Value justification, Greed, Authority, Satisfaction conviction, Curiosity, Urgency, Exclusivity, Simplicity, Specificity, Hope

### Robert Cialdini — 6 Principles of Persuasion
1. Reciprocity (give first, ask second)
2. Commitment & Consistency (small yeses → big yeses)
3. Social Proof (people check reviews before buying)
4. Authority (expertise signals)
5. Liking (people buy from people they like)
6. Scarcity (limited time/quantity — must be REAL)

### Claude Hopkins — Scientific Advertising
- Advertising is salesmanship; test everything
- Specificity crushes vague claims
- Tell the full story; appeal to self-interest

## 4.3 Content Strategy

### Content Pillars
- 3-5 core themes governing all content output
- Calendar: 70% planned pillar, 20% reactive/trending, 10% experimental
- Distribution mix: 40% Educational, 25% Inspirational, 20% Promotional, 15% Brand Story

### The 80/20 Distribution Rule
- Spend 80% of time DISTRIBUTING, 20% creating
- Gary Vee Pyramid: 1 pillar piece → 30-60 micro-content pieces across all channels

### Content Repurposing (1 → 12+)
Blog post → LinkedIn post → X thread → Short-form video → Email newsletter → Podcast → Infographic → Carousel → Quote graphics → PDF checklist → Email sequence → Show notes

## 4.4 Headline & Hook Mastery

### 10 Highest-Converting Headline Formulas
1. How-To: "How to [Outcome] Without [Pain]"
2. Number: "[N] [Things] to [Outcome]"
3. Specific Result: "How [Entity] Got [Result] in [Timeframe]"
4. Warning: "Warning: [Mistake] Is [Costing You]"
5. Question: "Are You [Making Mistake]?"
6. Secret: "The [Secret] [Authority] [Discovered]"
7. Comparison: "[Old Approach] vs [New]: [Difference]"
8. Benefit + Time: "Get [Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
9. Curiosity Gap: "[Intriguing Statement Requiring Context]"
10. Personalized: "For [Person] Who Wants [Outcome]"

### Video Hooks (0-3 seconds)
- Question hook, Shocking fact, Bold statement, Relatable setup, Curiosity/cliffhanger, Tutorial

## 4.5 Content for Each Funnel Stage

### TOFU (Awareness)
- Educate first, sell never; optimized for discovery
- Blog posts, short-form video, infographics, threads, podcast appearances

### MOFU (Consideration)
- Compare and contrast; use social proof heavily
- Webinars, case studies, comparison guides, white papers, retargeting ads

### BOFU (Decision)
- Eliminate risk, create urgency, address final objections
- Landing pages, free trials, testimonials, ROI calculators, personalized emails

## 4.6 The Pre-Write Checklist
- [ ] Who is the ONE reader?
- [ ] What awareness level? (Schwartz 1-5)
- [ ] What ONE action do I want?
- [ ] What is the #1 objection?
- [ ] What is the strongest proof?
- [ ] What emotional state am I creating?
- [ ] Which framework fits?
- [ ] Which Cialdini principle can I activate?
- [ ] What is the most specific, quantified claim?

# 5. SALES ALIGNMENT & LEAD GEN

## 5.1 Lead Generation

### Channel Strategy Principles
- Inbound (SEO/Content): High quality, moderate cost
- Webinars: Very high quality, moderate cost
- Cold Email: Medium quality, low cost
- LinkedIn Outbound: High quality, higher cost
- Referral: Very high quality, near-zero cost
- Chatbot: High quality, moderate cost

### Lead Magnet Rankings (by conversion effectiveness)
1. Interactive Quizzes (highest CVR)
2. ROI/Cost Calculators (drives significant deal volume)
3. Checklists/Cheat Sheets (high on dedicated pages)
4. Templates
5. Free Tools/Mini-Apps
6. Webinars (live)
7. Ebooks/Whitepapers (declining)

### Landing Page Principles
- Video can significantly lift conversion
- Social proof produces meaningful conversion lift
- Form reduction is the highest-impact A/B test type
- Page speed improvements directly increase conversions

### Cold Email Principles
- Subject: 36-50 chars, personalized for higher response
- Body: 50-125 words; single low-friction CTA
- Sequence: 4-7 touchpoints; most replies on email 1
- Best send: mid-week, morning
- Deliverability: SPF/DKIM/DMARC + warm up gradually

## 5.2 Lead Qualification

### MQL vs SQL vs PQL
- PQLs convert at dramatically higher rates than MQLs
- Healthy MQL→SQL conversion: see benchmarks

### Lead Scoring (Two Dimensions)
- Fit Score (demographic/firmographic): title, company size, industry, geography
- Engagement Score (behavioral): demo requests (high), pricing visits (medium), content downloads (low)
- AI-driven scoring significantly outperforms static models

### Qualification Frameworks
| Framework | Best For | ACV |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| BANT | Transactional/velocity | <$25K |
| CHAMP | Mid-market consultative | $25-100K |
| MEDDPICC | Enterprise/multi-stakeholder | $100K+ |

## 5.3 Nurture Sequences

### Standard B2B Nurture (MQL → SQL): 7 emails over 30 days
1. Day 0: Welcome + deliver lead magnet
2. Day 3: Educational — address #1 pain
3. Day 7: Social proof — customer story
4. Day 12: Value differentiation
5. Day 17: Objection handling
6. Day 22: Soft offer
7. Day 30: Direct ask

### Key Principles
- Segmented emails dramatically outperform batch-and-blast
- Multi-channel nurture outperforms single-channel

## 5.4 Sales Enablement

### Battle Card Template
- Competitor overview + Their strengths + Their weaknesses
- Our differentiators (3-5 provable points)
- Landmine questions + "When they say X, you say Y"
- Win stories + Loss patterns

### ROI Calculators
- Show current cost FIRST (loss aversion), then savings

### Sales-Marketing SLA
- Fast MQL contact dramatically increases conversion likelihood
- Aligned teams produce higher win rates and faster revenue growth

## 5.5 Revenue Operations (RevOps)

### Pipeline Velocity
```
Pipeline Velocity = (Opportunities x Avg Deal Size x Win Rate) / Sales Cycle Days
```

### 12 Core RevOps KPIs
Pipeline velocity, MQL→SQL rate, SQL→Opp rate, Win rate, Average deal size, Sales cycle length, CAC, CLTV, NRR, Forecast accuracy, Marketing-sourced revenue %, Pipeline coverage ratio

## 5.6 Account-Based Marketing (ABM)

### Three Tiers
| Tier | Accounts | ACV | Personalization |
|------|----------|-----|-----------------|
| 1:1 Strategic | 5-20 | $500K+ | Fully custom |
| 1:Few Scaled | 20-200 | $50-250K | Industry-specific |
| 1:Many Programmatic | 200-5,000 | <$50K | Role/segment |

### ABM Principles
- ABM programs drive the majority of enterprise opportunities and revenue
- Shared KPI contracts between sales and marketing accelerate conversion
- ABM produces significantly higher win rates

## 5.7 Pricing Psychology
1. **Anchoring**: Show higher price first
2. **Decoy Effect**: Users tend to choose middle of 3 tiers
3. **Charm Pricing**: $99 vs $100 (left-digit bias)
4. **Loss Framing**: Frame as cost of NOT upgrading
5. **Granularity**: Per-day feels less than per-month

### Free Trial Models
- Opt-out (CC required): highest conversion
- Opt-in (no CC): moderate conversion
- Freemium → Paid: lowest conversion

# 6. AI-POWERED MARKETING

## 6.1 AI Content Generation — Principles

### HITL Quality Workflow
Strategist (brief) → AI (draft) → Content Lead (review) → AI (refine) → Editor (polish) → Legal (spot-check)
- Saves significant time while maintaining brand quality

### Tool Selection Criteria
- Long-form / enterprise: brand IQ, content pipelines
- Brand consistency / nuance: complex brand guideline adherence
- Versatile / rapid: wide plugin ecosystem
- Ad creatives / short-form: speed and variant generation

### AI Image Selection Criteria
- Photorealistic product/lifestyle: GPT Image generation
- Artistic / editorial / brand aesthetic: Midjourney-class tools
- Volume generation with custom models: Stable Diffusion-class tools
- Commercial-safe with clear licensing: Adobe Firefly-class tools

### AI Video Selection Criteria
- Brand films / VFX: Runway-class tools
- Cinematic campaigns: Sora-class tools
- Short-form social: Pika-class tools
- Personalized sales videos / multilingual: HeyGen-class tools

## 6.2 Marketing Automation — Principles

### Platform Selection Criteria
- Mid-market, CRM-centric: HubSpot-class
- SMB, advanced automation: ActiveCampaign-class
- Enterprise B2B: Marketo/Adobe-class
- Enterprise omnichannel: Salesforce-class

### Automation Connective Tissue
- Simple multi-app connections: Zapier-class (broad app coverage)
- Complex multi-step visual workflows: Make-class
- Open-source / AI-native workflows: n8n-class

## 6.3 AI-Powered Personalization — Principles
- Consumers expect personalized content
- Personalized websites significantly increase revenue
- AI personalization produces outsized ROI

### CDP Stack Selection Criteria
- Developer-first: Twilio Segment-class
- Mobile-first: mParticle-class
- Enterprise: Adobe/Salesforce-class

### Recommendation Engines
- AI recommendations drive a substantial portion of e-commerce sales

## 6.4 Programmatic & AI Advertising — Principles

### AI-Driven Creative Optimization
- Brands providing maximum creative assets see significantly better performance
- DCO (Dynamic Creative Optimization) improves ROAS and reduces CPA

### Lookalike Audiences
- AI analyzes hundreds of behavioral/psychographic traits

## 6.5 AI Agents in Marketing — Principles

### Agent Types
1. **Listener Agents** — Monitor calls, mentions, feedback 24/7
2. **Topic/Research Agents** — Generate content ideas from listener data
3. **Creator Agents** — Draft marketing assets maintaining brand voice
4. **Optimization Agents** — Monitor and adjust campaigns autonomously
5. **Outreach Agents** — Personalize sequences, qualify leads, schedule meetings

### Multi-Agent Architecture
```
ORCHESTRATOR (Goal: "Increase organic leads 20%")
  ├── RESEARCH AGENT (competitors, trends, gaps)
  ├── CONTENT AGENT (blogs, social, email)
  ├── SEO AGENT (GEO, technical, backlinks)
  ├── DISTRIBUTION AGENT (schedule, publish)
  ├── PAID AGENT (run and optimize ads)
  └── ANALYTICS AGENT (monitor, report, feed learnings)
```

### The AI Maturity Scale
- Level 0: AI-Assisted (humans use AI tools for individual tasks)
- Level 1: AI-Augmented (AI handles first drafts, humans review)
- Level 2: AI-Automated (AI handles workflows, humans set strategy)
- Level 3: AI-Native (multi-agent systems run campaigns autonomously)


# 7. MARKET RESEARCH & COMPETITIVE INTEL

## 7.1 Research Methodologies

### Primary Research
- **Surveys**: large sample for consumer, moderate sample for B2B for statistical significance
- **IDIs**: 15-30 interviews per segment before saturation
- **Focus Groups**: 4-12 participants; risk of groupthink
- **Ethnographic**: dramatically higher product success rates

### Secondary Research Sources
- Premium: IBISWorld, Euromonitor, Gartner, Forrester, CB Insights
- Free: Statista, Census Bureau, FRED, SEC EDGAR, Google Scholar, McKinsey Insights

### TAM/SAM/SOM
- Top-Down: Industry report → filter by geography/segment
- Bottom-Up (preferred): # target customers x ARPU = TAM
- Investors prefer bottom-up because it's rooted in unit economics

## 7.2 Competitive Analysis Frameworks

### Porter's Five Forces
Threat of new entrants, Supplier power, Buyer power, Substitutes, Rivalry
- Score each 1-5; lower total = more attractive market

### SWOT → Strategic Moves
- S+O → Leverage; S+T → Mitigate; W+O → Improve; W+T → Avoid

### PESTEL
Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal
- Feeds Opportunities and Threats quadrants of SWOT

### Win/Loss Analysis
- Interview within 2-4 weeks of deal decision
- 20-30 interviews for patterns; 50+ for stability
- Price is cited as loss driver frequently, but is actually primary driver much less often

## 7.3 Competitive Intelligence Gathering

### CI Platform Selection Criteria
- Product + marketing teams: Kompyte-class
- Sales enablement: Klue-class
- Broadest data coverage: Crayon-class
- Web traffic benchmarking: SimilarWeb-class

### Technology Stack Analysis
- Use tools like BuiltWith (historical) + Wappalyzer (real-time)
- Detects: CMS, marketing automation, analytics, support tools, A/B testing, payment processors

### Review Mining (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius)
- Extract: feature gaps, pain points, switching reasons, competitive comparisons
- AI-process large review sets for theme extraction
- Monitor frequently per competitor

### Job Posting Analysis
- Engineering surge → product acceleration
- Legal/compliance surge → regulatory preparation
- Enterprise sales hires → upmarket move
- Geographic role expansion → market entry signal

## 7.4 Trend Detection

### Signal Hierarchy
| Strength | Lead Time | Sources |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Very Weak | 18-36 months | Academic papers, patents |
| Weak | 12-18 months | Niche newsletters, Reddit/HN, early VC |
| Emerging | 6-12 months | Product Hunt, tech press |
| Mainstream | 0-6 months | Mass media, Google Trends surge |

### Weak Signal Detection
1. Scan broadly (50+ sources)
2. Filter by novelty
3. Assess plausibility
4. Cross-validate (2+ independent sources)
5. Score impact potential
6. Track velocity (frequency increasing?)
7. Build scenarios

## 7.5 Segmentation Models

| Model | Basis | Best For |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Demographic | Age, gender, income | Consumer positioning |
| Psychographic | Values, attitudes, lifestyles | Brand messaging |
| Behavioral | Purchase history, usage | Lifecycle campaigns |
| Firmographic | Company size, industry, revenue | ABM |
| Technographic | Tech stack used | SaaS lead targeting |
| RFM | Recency, Frequency, Monetary | Budget prioritization |

### RFM Segments
- Champions (5/5/5): Reward, referral programs
- At-Risk (2-3/3-5/3-5): Win-back campaigns
- Lost Champions (1-2/4-5/4-5): Aggressive re-engagement
- Hibernating (1-2/1-2/1-2): Low-cost reactivation or suppress

## 7.6 Data-Driven Decision Making

### Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM)
- Privacy-safe, cookie-independent — experiencing a renaissance
- Open-source Bayesian MMM tools available (e.g., Google Meridian)
- Requires 6-12 months data; outputs budget allocation + channel ROI + saturation curves

### Conjoint Analysis
- Choice-Based Conjoint (CBC): industry standard for pricing/feature research
- Determines exact willingness to pay per feature bundle

### LTV Calculation
```
Simple: LTV = ARPU x Gross Margin % x (1 / Churn Rate)
```

### LTV:CAC Interpretation
| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| < 1:1 | Destroying value |
| 3:1 | Healthy benchmark |
| 4-7:1 | Strong profitability |
| > 10:1 | Under-investing or dominant moat |

# QUICK REFERENCE: THE MARKETING AGENT'S DECISION TREE

```
1. DOES PMF EXIST?
   → Run Sean Ellis 40% test
   → Below 40%: FIX PRODUCT, don't scale marketing
   → Above 40%: SCALE AGGRESSIVELY

2. WHAT'S THE BIGGEST FUNNEL BOTTLENECK?
   → Measure AARRR conversion rates at each stage
   → Find biggest drop-off → that's your #1 priority

3. WHAT GROWTH LOOPS CAN WE BUILD?
   → Map user actions to potential loop mechanics
   → Test loops with K > 0.3

4. WHAT SHOULD WE TEST?
   → Generate hypotheses from behavior data
   → Score with ICE/RICE
   → Run highest-scoring first
   → One NSM per stage, one test at a time

5. ARE WE RETAINING?
   → Check Day 7/30 retention curves
   → Curves → zero: fix product
   → Curves flatten: scale acquisition

6. WHICH COPY FRAMEWORK?
   → Cold audience, short: PAS
   → Warm audience, transformation: BAB
   → High-ticket, consultative: QUEST
   → Product launch: 4Ps
   → Sales page: AIDA + Schwartz awareness level
```
